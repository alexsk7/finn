What it covers

One quiet place for the financial system you already have.

finn is built for people who want a practical operating view of their money without handing the whole picture to another cloud service.

DashboardNet worth, trends, allocation, cashflow, alerts, and market context.
AccountsLive balances from transactions and holdings, grouped by type.
BudgetingZero-based monthly planning, actuals, variance, and uncategorized cleanup.
InvestmentsHoldings, asset classes, allocation targets, and portfolio drift.
Tax & TLHUnrealized gains, YTD income, and harvestable loss candidates.
RebalancingTax-aware sell priorities and new-cash scenarios.
Real estateEquity, LTV, appreciation, amortization, and capex tracking.
CSV importsBring in bank transactions, holdings, and historical snapshots.
PortfoliosSeparate SQLite files for households, scenarios, or long-running records.
BackupsStartup and on-demand local backups using SQLite's safe backup API.
Why local-first?

Your financial record should be durable, inspectable, and yours.

Local-first means finn runs on your machine and keeps its working data in SQLite files you can back up, move, inspect, and keep for the long term.

There is no account to create, no ad network to feed, no hosted profile to monetize, and no subscription gate between you and your own history.

Price refreshes can use Yahoo Finance when available, but the core app keeps working offline. Your financial system remains useful even when the network is not.

Cloud finance apps

Often optimized around accounts, sync, retention, referrals, ads, and recurring revenue.

finn

Optimized around ownership, clarity, local files, explicit imports, and practical planning.

AUM models

Can be valuable for advice, but even small asset-based fees compound over time.

Why support finn?

Free and open source should still be cared for.

finn has no subscription, no managed account, and no assets-under-management fee. Supporting the project helps sustain development, documentation, import polish, and the careful maintenance that finance software deserves.

The point is not to replace every advisor or every spreadsheet. It is to give capable people a calm, transparent system they can own.

Getting started

Clone it, run it, keep your data local.

Requires uv and Python 3.12+. Demo data loads on first run so the app is not empty.

cd finn
./run.sh
